# Tensa — Product Context

**register: product**

## Product Purpose
Tensa is a self-hosted, ad-free media toolkit. You paste a URL (YouTube, Instagram,
TikTok, Facebook, X) or upload a file, and get it back as video/audio, extracted
audio, a converted format, or a transcript — with live progress and no ad-plagued,
malware-adjacent download sites in between. The whole point is that it does one job
cleanly and stays out of your way.

## Users
Technically literate people running this on their own machine or homelab: developers,
self-hosters, people who already know what a `cookies.txt` is. They are comfortable
pasting a URL and reading a progress bar. They do not need hand-holding, marketing
fluff, or persuasion — they already chose this tool. They value speed, clarity, and
the feeling of a precise instrument.

## Tone
Confident, quiet, exact. The voice of a good command-line tool that grew a face.
Lowercase-comfortable. No exclamation, no hype, no "🚀". It states what it does and
shows progress honestly.

## Anti-references (what it must NOT look like)
- The ad-plagued download sites it replaces (savefrom, vidssave, y2mate): cluttered,
  banner-stuffed, fake-button, untrustworthy.
- A generic create-next-app + shadcn starter: centered white card, default emerald
  green, Geist/Inter, "clean & professional" with no point of view. (This is exactly
  what the previous version looked like, and it was rejected.)
- SaaS landing-page tropes: hero metrics, identical feature-card grids, gradient text.

## Strategic principles
- The URL input is the product. It should feel like the primary, almost magnetic
  surface — paste, hit go, watch it work.
- Progress is the second hero. Downloads in flight should feel alive and legible.
- Density over emptiness: this is a working tool, not a brochure. But density with
  rhythm, not clutter.
- Earn trust through restraint and precision, the opposite of the sites it replaces.

## Aesthetic direction (confirmed with user)
**Dark cinematic.** A moody, near-black tinted surface; the interface feels like a
premium media application. One vivid accent that glows, used sparingly on the live/
active elements (the go action, in-flight progress). Subtle, purposeful depth — not
decorative glassmorphism everywhere. A characterful display typeface for "Tensa" and
headings; absolutely not Geist or Inter.
